<p><p>The natural polymer market in 3D printed brain models consists of materials derived from biological sources like proteins, polysaccharides, or DNA. These polymers offer biocompatibility and can accurately mimic the structure and functions of the brain. On the other hand, the synthetic polymer market includes materials like polylactic acid (PLA) or polyethylene terephthalate (PET). These polymers provide durability, flexibility, and are cost-effective for creating brain models. Both the natural and synthetic polymer markets cater to the growing demand for realistic and customizable brain models for medical education, research, and surgical planning.</p></p>
