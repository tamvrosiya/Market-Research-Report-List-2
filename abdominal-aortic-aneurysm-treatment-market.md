<p><p>Abdominal aortic aneurysm (AAA) treatment includes both non-surgical and surgical approaches to manage the condition. Non-surgical treatment options involve monitoring the size of the aneurysm regularly with imaging tests and maintaining a healthy lifestyle to control blood pressure and reduce the risk of rupture. Surgical treatment options are typically recommended when the aneurysm reaches a significant size or the risk of rupture becomes high. These surgical approaches include open surgery and endovascular aneurysm repair (EVAR).</p><p>Open surgery involves replacing the damaged portion of the aorta with a synthetic graft through a large incision in the abdomen. EVAR is a less invasive procedure where a stent graft is placed inside the aneurysm through small incisions in the groin. This procedure reduces the risk of complications associated with open surgery.</p><p>The abdominal aortic aneurysm treatment market is expected to experience significant growth during the forecast period. This growth can be attributed to several factors. Firstly, the rising prevalence of cardiovascular diseases, including AAA, across the globe, is driving the demand for treatment options. Additionally, technological advancements in surgical procedures, such as the development of fenestrated stent grafts for complex aneurysms, are further fueling the market growth.</p><p>Furthermore, increasing awareness about early diagnosis and screening programs for AAA, coupled with favorable reimbursement policies, is expected to drive the market growth. The implementation of minimally invasive procedures like EVAR also contributes to the increasing adoption of AAA treatment options. With the aging population worldwide, the demand for AAA treatment is anticipated to rise in the coming years.</p><p>Overall, the abdominal aortic aneurysm treatment market is projected to grow at a CAGR of 12.7% during the forecast period, driven by the increasing prevalence of AAA, technological advancements, and greater emphasis on early diagnosis and treatment.</p></p>
