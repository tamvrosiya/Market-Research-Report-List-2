<p><p>Biophotonics technology encompasses various applications. See-through imaging allows visualization of objects, tissues, or organs concealed behind biological materials. Inside imaging involves non-invasive imaging of internal structures. Spectro molecular techniques use light to analyze biological molecules and their functional characteristics. Surface imaging captures detailed images of surfaces at the microscopic level. Microscopy employs light to visualize tiny structures in biological samples. Light therapy utilizes light sources to treat various medical conditions. Analytical sensing involves detecting and monitoring biological substances. Biosensors use light to detect and analyze biological components for medical or diagnostic purposes. These applications enable advancements in healthcare, research, and diagnostics.</p></p>
