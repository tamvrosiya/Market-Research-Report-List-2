<p><p>Bullous pemphigoid is a chronic autoimmune skin disorder that is characterized by the development of large, fluid-filled blisters on the skin. It is primarily treated with medications to control and suppress the immune system's response. The goal of treatment is to alleviate the symptoms, promote healing of blisters, and prevent relapses.</p><p>The mainstay of bullous pemphigoid treatment is corticosteroids, which can be administered topically, orally, or intravenously. Topical corticosteroids are commonly used for mild cases, while systemic corticosteroids are prescribed for moderate to severe cases. In some cases, additional immunosuppressive medications such as azathioprine or mycophenolate mofetil may be used as steroid-sparing agents or in cases where corticosteroids alone are not effective enough.</p><p>In recent years, there have been several advancements in the treatment of bullous pemphigoid.
