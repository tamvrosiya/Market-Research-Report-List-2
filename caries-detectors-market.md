<p><p>Caries detectors are used in dentistry to detect tooth decay and diagnose the extent of cavities. One type of caries detector is transillumination, which involves shining a light through the tooth to identify any changes in its structure. Another type is laser fluorescence, which uses a laser to detect the presence of bacteria or decay in the tooth. Both types are non-invasive and provide quick and accurate results, aiding in early detection and effective treatment of dental caries.</p></p>
