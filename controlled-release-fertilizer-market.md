<p><p>Controlled-release fertilizer, also known as slow-release or timed-release fertilizer, is a type of plant nutrient that releases nutrients gradually over an extended period. It consists of coated or encapsulated granules which break down and release nutrients into the soil based on specific conditions such as temperature, moisture, and bacterial activity, to ensure a consistent supply of nutrients to the plants. This helps to minimize nutrient loss, improve crop yield and quality, and reduce the frequency of fertilizer application.</p><p>The global controlled-release fertilizer market is expected to witness significant growth in the coming years. The increasing demand for high-efficiency fertilizers that reduce environmental pollution and ensure sustainable agriculture practices is one of the major factors driving the market growth. Additionally, the growing adoption of advanced farming techniques and technologies, such as precision agriculture, is further fueling the market expansion.</p><p>The market is also witnessing several trends that are shaping its growth trajectory. One such trend is the increasing use of polymer-coated controlled-release fertilizers, which provide a more controlled nutrient release and offer enhanced nutrient availability to plants. Another trend is the rising popularity of blended controlled-release fertilizers, which combine both slow-release and quick-release nutrients to meet the specific nutrient requirements of different crops and soils.</p><p>Moreover, the market is experiencing a shift towards organic and bio-based controlled-release fertilizers, as they are considered environmentally friendly and promote sustainable farming practices. These products are gaining traction due to the rising consumer awareness regarding the harmful effects of chemical fertilizers on soil health and water quality.</p><p>Overall, the controlled-release fertilizer market is projected to experience steady growth, with an expected CAGR of 6% during the forecast period. The market's expansion is driven by the increasing demand for efficient and sustainable agricultural practices, as well as the adoption of advanced farming technologies and the growing popularity of organic and bio-based fertilizers.</p></p>

<p><p>The controlled-release fertilizer market consists of three main types: organic polymer coated, inorganic sulfur, and combined inorganic and organic fertilizers. Organic polymer coated fertilizers are made by coating conventional fertilizers with a polymer membrane, providing slow nutrient release. Inorganic sulfur fertilizers release nutrients gradually by oxidation of elemental sulfur. Combined inorganic and organic fertilizers are blends of both inorganic and organic materials, providing a balanced and sustained nutrient release. These types of controlled-release fertilizers are designed to improve nutrient efficiency, reduce environmental impacts, and ensure long-lasting plant nutrition.</p></p>
