<p><ul><li>Floating Electrode Catheter</li><li>Common Electrode Catheter</li></ul></p>
<p><p>The Electrode Catheter Market consists of two main types: Floating Electrode Catheter and Common Electrode Catheter. </p><p>Floating Electrode Catheters are designed with a floating electrode at the tip, which allows for optimal contact with the tissue during procedures like ablation. This type of catheter enhances the accuracy of electrical signal detection and minimizes tissue damage.</p><p>On the other hand, Common Electrode Catheters have fixed electrodes throughout their length. These catheters are more commonly used for diagnostic purposes like mapping the heart's electrical activity or measuring pressure. They provide a stable and consistent electrical signal pickup from various locations within the heart.</p></p>
