<p><p>Emergency external defibrillators are medical devices used to deliver an electric shock to the heart in case of cardiac arrest. There are two types available in the market: semi-automated and fully automated. Semi-automated defibrillators require the user to interpret the heart rhythm through electrodes and manually deliver a shock if necessary, whereas fully automated defibrillators automatically analyze the heart rhythm and deliver a shock without the need for user intervention. Both types are essential for prompt response in emergencies and can potentially save lives by restoring normal heart rhythms.</p></p>
