<p><p>An evaporative condensing unit is a type of refrigeration system that uses the evaporative cooling process to remove heat from the condenser. It consists of a compressor, a condenser, a fan, and an evaporative cooler. The compressor compresses the refrigerant, which then flows into the condenser where it releases heat. The fan blows air over the condenser coils to aid in heat transfer. The evaporative cooler uses water or a combination of water and air to cool the refrigerant and increase its efficiency.</p><p>The evaporative condensing unit market is expected to grow at a CAGR of 12.9% during the forecast period.
