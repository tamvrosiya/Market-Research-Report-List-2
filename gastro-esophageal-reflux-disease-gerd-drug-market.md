<p><p>The Gastro Esophageal Reflux Disease (GERD) drug market consists of three main types: H2 receptor antagonists, proton pump inhibitors (PPi), and antacids. H2 receptor antagonists work by reducing stomach acid production, while PPi medications also block acid production but in a different way. Antacids, on the other hand, neutralize stomach acid. These drugs are commonly used to alleviate the symptoms of GERD, such as heartburn. Understanding the different types of GERD drugs helps healthcare professionals determine the most appropriate treatment for patients.</p></p>
