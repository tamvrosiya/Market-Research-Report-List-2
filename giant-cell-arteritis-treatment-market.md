<p><p>Giant cell arteritis (GCA) is a chronic inflammatory disease affecting the large and medium-sized arteries, primarily those in the head and neck. If left untreated, GCA can lead to serious complications, including vision loss and strokes. The main treatment for GCA is high-dose corticosteroids, such as prednisone, which help reduce inflammation and prevent damage to blood vessels. However, long-term use of corticosteroids can result in various side effects, making it necessary to gradually taper the dosage over time.</p><p>In recent years, there has been a growing demand for alternative treatments that can help reduce the reliance on corticosteroids or provide more effective disease control. As a result, various targeted immunotherapies and biologic agents have emerged as potential treatment options. These include tocilizumab, an interleukin-6 receptor inhibitor, and ustekinumab, an interleukin-12 and interleukin-23 inhibitor.</p><p>The global giant cell arteritis treatment market is expected to witness steady growth in the coming years. <p><p>The Giant Cell Arteritis Treatment market includes two main types - Corticosteroid Therapy and Adjuvant Agents. Corticosteroid Therapy involves the use of strong anti-inflammatory drugs, such as prednisone, to manage the symptoms and reduce inflammation in the blood vessels affected by the disease. Adjuvant Agents are additional medications used in combination with corticosteroids to enhance their effectiveness and reduce the risk of side effects.
