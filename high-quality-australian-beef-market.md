<p><p>The Australian beef market is divided into two main types: grainfed beef and grassfed beef. Grainfed beef refers to cattle that are raised on a diet supplemented with grain and other feed, typically in feedlots. It is known for its marbling, tenderness, and rich flavor. Grassfed beef, on the other hand, comes from cattle that feed exclusively on grass throughout their lives. It has a leaner and slightly gamier taste compared to grainfed beef. Both types of beef are popular in Australia and cater to different consumer preferences.</p></p>
