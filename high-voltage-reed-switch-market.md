<p><p>The High Voltage Reed Switch market consists of three primary types: Form A, Form B, and Form C. Form A refers to a normally open reed switch, meaning that the switch is closed in its natural state and only opens when a magnetic field is present. Form B, on the other hand, is normally closed, meaning that the switch is closed until a magnetic field is applied, causing it to open. Lastly, Form C is a changeover switch that has both normally open and normally closed contacts, providing flexibility in different electrical circuit applications.</p></p>
