<p><p>Utility-owned Model: In this market type, the utility company owns and manages the energy storage system (ESS). They deploy the ESS on their premises or at specific locations to support their grid operations. The utility company has full control over the ESS and utilizes it to enhance grid reliability, reduce peak demand, and optimize their overall energy management.</p><p>Customer-owned Model: In this market type, individual customers or businesses own and operate ESS on their premises or facilities. They deploy ESS to reduce energy costs through peak shaving, load shifting, or backup power supply. The customers have control over the ESS and benefit from improved energy efficiency and sustainability within their premises.</p><p>Third-party-owned Model: In this market type, a third-party company owns and manages ESS independently of the utility or the customers. They enter into agreements with utilities or customers to install and operate ESS on their premises. The third-party company typically bears the upfront costs, while the utilities or customers pay for the storage services provided, such as peak shaving or demand response.</p></p>
