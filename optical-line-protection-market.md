<p><p>Optical Line Protection refers to a mechanism used in telecommunications networks to ensure uninterrupted data transmission. The 1+1 Optical Line Protection market type involves duplicating the transmission path, wherein two identical optical paths are created, and if one fails, the other becomes active instantly. On the other hand, the 1:1 Optical Line Protection market type provides a backup path for the main transmission line, allowing for a seamless switch in case of a failure. Both types aim to enhance network reliability and minimize service disruptions.</p></p>
