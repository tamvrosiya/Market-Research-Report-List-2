<p><p>Revision knee systems are medical devices used in orthopedic surgery to replace all or part of the knee joint when it has become damaged or deteriorated. These systems consist of different components that are designed to replace the femoral (thigh bone), tibia (shin bone), and patella (kneecap) parts of the knee. The femoral component is the part that attaches to the thigh bone, the tibial component replaces the shin bone, and the patellar component replaces the kneecap. Each component plays a crucial role in restoring function and stability to the knee joint during revision surgery.</p></p>
