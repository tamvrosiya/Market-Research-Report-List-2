<p><p>Thermopile and microbolometer infrared detectors are two types of sensors used in infrared thermal imaging systems. These detectors work by converting thermal energy into electrical signals, allowing the detection and measurement of infrared radiation.</p><p>The thermopile infrared detector consists of multiple thermocouples connected in series or parallel. It operates based on the principle of the Seebeck effect, where a temperature difference between two metals generates an electric voltage. Thermopile detectors are commonly used in non-contact temperature measurement devices and gas analysis applications.</p><p>On the other hand, a microbolometer infrared detector consists of an array of tiny resistive elements that change their electrical resistance with temperature. These detectors are usually made from vanadium oxide or amorphous silicon and are widely used in commercial thermal imaging cameras and firefighting equipment.</p><p>The thermopile and microbolometer infrared detector market is expected to witness steady growth during the forecast period. Factors driving this growth include the increasing adoption of infrared thermal imaging technology in various industries such as aerospace, defense, automotive, and healthcare. The demand for these detectors is further boosted by the rising need for non-destructive testing and preventive maintenance.</p><p>In addition, advancements in microbolometer technology, such as higher resolutions and improved image quality, are also contributing to market growth.
