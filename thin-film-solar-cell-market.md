<p><p>The thin film solar cell market is dominated by three major types: CdTe, CIGS, and GaAs. CdTe (Cadmium Telluride) type cells are made by depositing a thin layer of cadmium telluride on a glass or flexible substrate. CIGS (Copper Indium Gallium Selenide) type cells use a combination of copper, indium, gallium, and selenium to create the thin film layer. GaAs (Gallium Arsenide) type cells utilize gallium arsenide as the semiconductor material. These types of thin film solar cells offer various advantages such as flexibility, cost-effectiveness, and high efficiency, catering to different market demands.</p></p>
